About

I was born in East Baltimore and came up through foster care. A bus ride downtown changed my life when I enrolled in Job Corps, which led to a high school diploma and a degree in chemistry. I became the first in my family to graduate college.

Today, I work in food safety and quality assurance at Ferrero. My focus is on identifying failure modes, closing quality gaps, and building systems that prevent problems before they happen. I hold a PCQI certification and have worked across pharmaceutical, chemical, and food manufacturing environments.

I also write about Black American identity, consciousness, and fatherhood on Medium.
